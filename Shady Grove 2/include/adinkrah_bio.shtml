Why did you choose UMBC-Shady Grove? 

I chose UMBC- Shady Grove because it is an Honor’s University . Being a student at UMBC-Shady Grove means I have some of the best professors in the country as my instructors. In addition, the UMBC- Shady Grove campus is very convenient for commuting purposes.  It is an institution that motivates and rewards students with all the resources and attention they need to succeed.

What internships and/or extracurricular activities have you been involved with at UMBC-Shady Grove?
Aside being an active member of the Peer Advisory Team (PAT) at UMBC- Shady Grove, I occasionally attend the Psychology Student Association (PSA) meetings on campus.

It is a well-organized group and I recommend it to all students majoring and minoring psychology. Occasionally, the group invites speakers to discuss employment opportunities for psychology majors and other current research.

What are your plans after graduation? 
I plan to obtain a steady job with benefits after graduation and then pursue a Master’s  in Divinity. I have many goals for the future and one of them is to become a counselor.  I hope to help those in need, be a light to others, and have fun.

What advice would you provide to a prospective student?
After celebrating the positive result of your admission into UMBC-Shady Grove, you may be wondering what is next.  My advice to all prospective students is to develop an idea of what classes you will need to take and do not pile too much on your plate during your first semester.  Take it easy and get acquainted with your program of study.  After your first semester you will be able to better figure out the number of credits you can take without getting overwhelmed.  Explore the campus, attend as many events as possible, be open to many groups and organizations, make lots of friends, and most importantly take your studies seriously.
