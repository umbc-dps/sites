Beya "Benny" Tshimuna

An interesting personal fact …

I was born in the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC), formerly known as Zaire. I speak French as well as two other local dialects. I have a great sense of humor and I rarely take myself too seriously unless I have to, of course, at which point I will switch on the “seriousness” in an instance. I think humor is the greatest remedy to world problems because if we can all put aside our differences and laugh together, we will discover that our commonalities supersede our differences.

Why did you choose UMBC-Shady Grove?

I chose UMBC-Shady Grove because it is closer to home so there is no need to commute a long distance. 

This is especially important for me because I work full-time and attend school full-time. The Universities at Shady Grove are by far the best option for working students, not only because of the sense of community, but also because of the many opportunities available to students such as internships with government institutions like the U.S. Department of State, large private companies like Morgan Stanley, non-profit organizations like United Way, think tanks like the Brookings Institute as well as lobbying firms, all within the DC, Maryland, and Virginia (DMV) area.

The faculty and staff members are the greatest I have yet to meet anywhere. The resources for success are endless and professors are readily available to assist students. I also wanted to attend school locally in order to engage in the local community and take advantage of the many opportunities our area has to offer. The Universities at Shady Grove, as a whole, has been heavily involved in our local community. Last year, for example, the Universities at Shady Grove hosted a Food Recovery Event with students from University of Maryland Eastern Shore (UMES) who collaborated with the Corporate Volunteer Council of Montgomery County to increase alertness on ways to prevent food waste.  

What do you like best about UMBC-Shady Grove?

I like the faculty and staff.  In addition, the buildings are awesome and clean and the technological resources are vast and easily accessible. It is also a community-based campus with well-established roots in Montgomery County. Overall, The Universities at Shady Grove provide a unique blend of nine University of Maryland System institutions with a wide array of programs and accessibility to the main campus of each institution.

What internships and/or extra-curricular activities have you been involved with at UMBC-Shady Grove?

This is my second semester at UMBC-Shady Grove and I am a new member of the UMBC Peer Advisory Team. I regularly attend Wipe-Out-Wednesday (WOW) headed by the Political Science Department Director, Dr. Sunil Dasgupta. WOW is an open forum platform where we discuss a wide array of issues -- anything from culinary to current political events. It is a forum widely dominated with debates and persuasion and it provides students the opportunity to have intellectual discussions with professors. It is a good way for students to get accustomed to participating in open forum debates and voicing their thoughts on political as well as historical matters. I am also looking to reactivate our Political Science Club here at the Shady Grove Campus as well as do some volunteer work during the upcoming summer break, all while completing an internship with a non-profit organization within the DMV area.

What are your plans after graduation?

I plan to attend graduate school for international development if I do not immediately enter the workforce. I want to study international development in order to provide my time and effort to help the many underprivileged individuals around the world who are not afforded the same opportunities we have here in the United States.  I hope to change the lives of individuals for the better along the way.

What advice would you provide to a prospective student?

My advice to prospective students is to have a plan in place before entering a four year institution. UMBC-Shady Grove is a great place to develop your skills and knowledge. Take advantage of all the opportunities and resources available to you as there are many and do not leave any stones unturned. UMBC-Shady Grove is a small community which provides students the advantage to make connections and build networks. You will find many students with similar interests who may eventually become your friends, as relationships forged today become lifetime friendships tomorrow. UMBC-Shady Grove has an environment that is designed for you to succeed, but you have to want to be successful because no one else will do the work for you. If you are serious and dedicated to your academics, you will never lack the necessary assistance for your success. Most importantly, plan ahead and you will get ahead.
