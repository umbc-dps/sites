Marina Bronstein

An interesting personal fact ... 
An interesting fact about me is that I write poetry and play basketball.

Why did you choose UMBC-Shady Grove?
I chose UMBC-Shady Grove because it is an Honors University and it enables me access to a high quality education that's just 20 minutes away from my home and my multiple jobs. Also, the class sizes at UMBC-Shady Grove are small and I appreciate the state-of-the-art facilities and equipment. It's a safe and very dynamic environment.  

What do you like best about UMBC-Shady Grove?
What I like about UMBC-Shady Grove is the location and the environment. There are so many resources that are available to students.

The faculty has an open door policy which means they are always there if you need them, enabling you to build a strong relationship. The class sizes are small so you don't just feel like just a number, but that you matter. Lastly, UMBC-Shady Grove is like my second home. I arrive on campus in the morning to work out, go to class and then at night I spend time in the library for late night study hours.                                                                                                           
What internships and/or extracurricular activities have you been involved with at UMBC-Shady Grove?
Currently, I am involved with the UMBC Peer Advisory Team (PAT). PAT members assist with recruitment events, provide tours of the Universities at Shady Grove campus and connect with prospective students interested in attending UMBC-Shady Grove. I am also part of The Universities at Shady Grove Student Event Board (SEB). SEB sponsors a variety of student events and activities. In the future, I hope to join the Psychology Student Association (PSA). 

What are your plans after graduation? 
I am a psychology major and hope to become a school counselor. I want to work with high school students. When I was in high school I had an amazing counselor who helped guide me through my high school experience as a student athlete. My main interest is to help students with special needs and to guide students through their high school experience in preparation for college. Lastly, I want to create a program through which kids with special needs are able to participate in physical activities and complete homework with peers who do not have special needs. I believe that everyone deserves a chance.  I believe it’s important that we as individuals “don't judge a book by its cover” because we often don't know or understand the life experience of others until we walk a day in their shoes. 

What advice would you provide to a prospective student?
 I would advise prospective students to take risks, use resources, and never be afraid to show people your true colors or who you are. It takes time to settle in, so don't rush. Get involved with school organizations. There are many useful resources here at UMBC-Shady Grove. Don't be afraid to ask for help. College is about finding who you are and what you want to pursue as a career.
