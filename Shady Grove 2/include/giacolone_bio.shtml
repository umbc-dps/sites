Sarah Giacalone

An interesting personal fact ... 
I am from Kansas, but no I did not grow up on a farm, and no my dog’s name is not Toto.  Also, I have been told my “accent” only comes out when I say certain words and phrases. 

Why did you choose UMBC-Shady Grove? 
For me, UMBC- Shady Grove was the obvious choice because of the convenient location and great reputation of academic excellence.  After meeting with the Social Work Program Director, Katie Morris, LCSW-C, I knew it was a great fit.  She made me feel welcomed and excited to be a part of such an amazing school and program.   I looked at a few other schools in the area, but I didn’t feel like any of them could offer me the resources and opportunities that I have here at UMBC-Shady Grove.

What do you like best about UMBC-Shady Grove? 
I love the small classes and the community I have been able to build here at UMBC-Shady Grove.  Being able to build relationships with my professors and see that they genuinely care about my success as a student and in the future has pushed me to expect more of myself.  Another great thing about UMBC-Shady Grove is the amazing facilities and resources like the Center for Academic Success and the Green Grove Café.  It’s really nice to not have to leave campus to get a delicious meal.

What internships and/or extracurricular activities have you been involved with at UMBC-Shady Grove?
I spent my first semester figuring out which student organizations and opportunities I thought I would like to be a part of.  Currently, I am involved in the Writing Fellows Program, where I assist peers with building proficient organizational writing skills.  Good writing skills are needed in almost every profession, but especially in social work.  It helps me know that not only am I being trained in my own writing, but I am helping other students develop their skills and techniques as well.  I am also a member of the Peer Advisory Team, and will be assisting with welcoming new and prospective students to UMBC-Shady Grove. Through the Peer Advisory Team, I hope to develop my leadership skills and build strong peer and faculty relationships.

What are your plans after graduation? 
After graduating, I am hoping to continue my education in the Master’s of Social Work program at University of Maryland Baltimore.  I would like to go on to obtain my clinical licensure in social work.

What advice would you provide to a prospective student?
The best advice I can give to a prospective student is to figure out what your goals are and then plan the steps needed to achieve those goals.  I knew I wanted to attend UMBC-Shady Grove for over a year before I was financially ready to go back to school full-time, but I kept taking the small steps that I knew would help me once I was there.  Once you’re here, put in the effort to build relationships with your peers and professors because it will make your time and experience at UMBC-Shady Grove even more memorable and beneficial.  The professors, whether full-time or adjunct, all have great experience in the fields we hope to be working in, so this is our opportunity to soak up their knowledge and experience!
