One interesting fact about yourself …
One interesting fact about me is that I love to smile.

Why did you choose UMBC-Shady Grove?
After graduating from the Mental Health program at Montgomery College, I had an urge to major in social work. I decided to speak with alumni of UMBC-Shady Grove. Each one of them was quick to praise and speak positively of the University because of their experiences as past students. With research, I discovered that UMBC-Shady Grove offers the best social work undergraduate program in the entire Washington Metropolitan area. More so, I also found out that UMBC–Shady Grove is a multicultural institution which implies that diversity is highly valued. As a future social worker, a multicultural environment is exactly what I need to learn more about other cultures in order to become effective in my practice. These findings propelled me to become a part of the UMBC-Shady Grove community. 

In what internships or extracurricular activities have you been involved at UMBC-Shady Grove?
Though there are several extracurricular activities at UMBC-Shady Grove. I decided to join the Social Work Student Association (SWSA), and Tau Sigma National Transfer Honor Society. These associations help to connect students to educational and social opportunities. They also enable students to participate in community outreach activities. Students can sometimes earn extra-credit when participating in some of these activities. Moreover, I am a UMBC Peer Advisory Team member (PAT) at UMBC-Shady Grove. The PAT has given me the opportunity to learn leadership skills and to help prospective students with their process of transition into UMBC-Shady Grove. The PAT provide continuous support and guidance to new students throughout their first semester at UMBC-Shady Grove to enhance their success. 

What are your plans following graduation?
After graduating, I intend to proceed with my studies at a graduate school to earn a Master‘s in Social Work. I would like to become a Licensed Clinical Social Worker (LCSW-C) in order to work with adolescents who have addiction issues. I also hope to work with senior citizens, though there is a possibility that my aspirations could change over time as I proceed with my education. I am very excited and open minded about the direction of my future with UMBC-Shady Grove’s social work program.

What advice would you provide to a prospective student?
Transitioning into a new environment can be very stressful for new students. It’s very easy to become confused and concerned about unforeseen factors. My advice to prospective students is that you should visit UMBC-Shady Grove in order to become more knowledgeable about your educational options and career. Moreover, you should make sure that your Common Application and documentation needed for scholarships is submitted in a timely period in order to receive the full financial benefits that are available to students. Lastly, once enrolled in classes at UMBC-Shady Grove, try to stay informed by becoming a part of the many associations and groups on campus. Also, checking your email regularly will help you avoid any chance of missing important deadlines or information that will facilitate your learning process.
