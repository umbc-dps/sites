I am trilingual as I speak Armenian, Russian and English - blame it on my passion for exploring new cultures!

Why did you choose UMBC-Shady Grove?
I chose UMBC-Shady Grove for many reasons. The smaller classes provide an intimate atmosphere and help me interact freely with professors and other students. The accessible parking lot and location allows for an easy, convenient commute. In addition, the vast variety of foods and drinks offered at the Green Grove Cafe during lunch hours curb my food cravings during long study hours. Another great resource is the Priddy Library which has extended hours during weekdays, allowing me to focus on studying without having to worry about the closing hours.

In what internships or extracurricular activities have you been involved at UMBC-Shady Grove?
Currently at UMBC-Shady Grove, I am involved with the UMBC Peer Advisory Team (PAT). The Peer Advisory Team is an organization at UMBC-Shady Grove that connects prospective students with current students as they move through the transfer process. The members assist with recruitment events, provide tours of the Universities at Shady Grove and connect with prospective students interested in attending UMBC-Shady Grove. I am also a member of the Executive Board of the Psychology Student Association, serving as the Project Manager. This role includes planning events throughout the year to help our community and organize study halls. Finally, I am also a part of Tau Sigma National Honors Societies Leadership Board. The group is open to all UMBC students depending on their GPA. The group focuses on supporting the UMBC-Shady Grove community and also reaches out to help our local community through fundraising and charitable events.

What are your plans following graduation?
After graduation I plan to apply to the Industrial Organizational Program here at UMBC - Shady Grove. This has been a long time dream for me ever since I started my journey to pursue psychology as an undergrad.

What advice would you provide to a prospective student?
For all prospective students, my advice is to take your time and research the different opportunities UMBC-Shady Grove has to offer.
